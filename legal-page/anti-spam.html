About Spam
Spam is an electronic junk mail newsgroup of irrelevant or unwanted messages, sent over the internet. The most common form of spam is email spam. The sending of Unsolicited Bulk Email ("UBE") is banned by all Internet service providers worldwide. Bulk means that a large collection of messages, all contain equally similar content.

If you are using our services for spreading bulk emails and commercial emails, you must read and follow this Anti-spam Policy.

For different organizations, the definition of spam is also different. Here are some examples of spam, which we consider under Spamming -

  1. To send out any irrelevant content.
  2. To post unrelated links to your website.
  3. A mail sent to an invalid email address which doesn't exist.
  4. Mail sent to an email address without a domain name.
  5. Any message that contains false information.
  6. Content that introduces viruses, harmful code on the internet.
  7. If your IP address was used by somebody for spam & you are using that IP address to send email, then that message is called spam.
